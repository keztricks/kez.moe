Kieran as a Manager: Hints, cheats & tips!

This is a little document to give those on my team an idea of what to expect, it's based on How to Rands.

Hello! If you're reading this then it probably means that I'm your line manager, lucky you, welcome to the team!

If you're new, it takes a little time to get to know each other, and this document isn't going to do that, but hopefully it will get us kick started.

If you've been on the team a while then this is hopefully a good reference for what you should be getting from me, so be sure to keep me honest!

#000 Tea, oat milk, no sugar.

Hints for rookies

#001 Take your time, we don't expect you to set the world on fire on your first day, if you're here you're good enough to be here, chat to people, see what they're up to, get up to speed with whats going on.

#002 Take notes, notes are great, you don't know what's important and what's not when getting started, do yourself a favour, write it all down, you'll thank yourself later!

#003 Ask questions, we don't bite! We love it when you hit up Google and solve your own problem, but we also love it when you ask us, and we get to feel super cool by helping out. Our young team is full of people who were in your shoes not to long ago, they're know how will be invaluable!

#004 Get involved, be it in a group discussion, a table football game, a lunch time run or Friday pub, whatever you fancy, we work super hard, and we have a good time doing it.

#005 Don't bring lunch on Friday, it's fish and chip Friday, and lunch is on the company, better get your order in!

Being on the Team

#006 I'm available whenever, okay, so I have some kind of a life, and I might be in a meeting, or in the pub, but drop me a Slack message any hour, when I see it I'll either be right there, or I'll arange a time.

#007 Sometimes I work daft hours [#017], you don't have too, of course, I appreciate it when you go the extra mile [#013], but it's a choice, if I message you out of hours and it's not about an outage [#013], don't feel obliged to reply.

#009 We try hard to be super flexible, take advantage of the flexi-time policy, ask me if you want to work from home, so long as you're getting work done, I'll probably say yes!

#010 1:1s are at least monthly, probably more often if you're new to the team. They can be as often as you like, or impromptu [#006] if you or I have something to discuss.

Kieran's Favourites

#011 We work best when we're honest and open with each other, in 1:1s and day to day, I'll give my all to help us get the best out of each other. To do this I hope you're open about how things are going. Honesty about your performance, our performance, how you feel about work, helps us both peroform at our best, and have a good time doing it.

#012 Soft skills are important, we need good technical skills, we are technical practicioners, but they are not the be all and end all, good communication, personal management and teamwork are qualities required for anybody working in our industry.

#013 I appreciate it when you go the extra mile, learning in your own time, working out of hours when there's a tight deadline or downtime, joining in socially with the team [#004].

#014 Mind your Ps & Qs, I value politeness, ask don't tell, say thanks, treat everyone you work with respect.

#015 Keep me honest, been too busy to help out a few times? moved your 1:1 repeatedly? not gotten around to that thing I promissed? I'm a human, and a busy one at that, tell me if I screw up, I'll get on it.

Kieran's Quirks

#016 I'm a bit daft, okay, maybe I'm a lot daft, you don't need to be serious to take work seriously, I appreciate a laugh, but I can probably be a bit much, tell me to shut up if you'd like me to shut up!

#017 I suffer from Crohns Disease, this means that sometimes I work around my body, starting early or late or working from home. If you can't find me I'm probably in the loo! If I don't seem my usual self I'm probably unwell. I don't like it beating me, and will work to make up. Crohns and Colitis UK

#018 My initial opinion isn't usually what I settle on, it can take me some time to settle on an opinion or idea, this means I've refined my view, not that I've forgotten what I said initially.

#019 I'm good at finding a middle ground, I feel a strength I have as a manager is in keeping everyone happy, spinning preverbial plates and finding a middle ground, this is how I like to work.

#020 I am not good at confrontation, if I feel I've upset, annoyed, or otherwised inconvenienced someone it will play on my mind.
